# Bike Buyers Analysis

## Overview
An end-to-end data analysis project using Microsoft Excel to explore customer demographics and identify patterns in bike purchasing decisions across different regions, age groups, and income levels.

## What I Did
- Cleaned and standardized raw customer data (1,000+ rows) including inconsistencies in marital status, gender, and income fields
- Created a working sheet with additional calculated columns including age brackets (Adolescent, Middle Aged, Old)
- Built pivot tables to analyze purchasing behavior across multiple dimensions
- Designed an interactive dashboard with slicers for filtering by marital status, education, and region

## Key Findings
- Middle-aged customers consistently show the highest bike purchase rates across all filters
- Higher income generally correlates with higher purchase likelihood, but this relationship varies by gender and region
- Among married customers, higher income strongly predicts bike purchase for both males and females
- Among single customers with a Bachelor's degree in Europe, the pattern reverses — females with lower income purchased more
- In the Pacific region with Graduate Degree holders, females who purchased bikes had significantly higher incomes (114,000 vs 92,500), while males showed the opposite pattern
- Customers with shorter commutes (0-1 miles) consistently show the highest bike purchase rates across all segments

## Tools Used
- Microsoft Excel (Data Cleaning, Pivot Tables, Charts, Slicers, Interactive Dashboard)

## Dataset
Customer demographic dataset including: Age, Gender, Income, Marital Status, Education, Occupation, Region, Commute Distance, Home Ownership, Bike Purchase Decision
